# Dog Care Best Practices

## Daily Exercise Requirements

### Puppies (0-1 year)
- **Duration**: 5 minutes per month of age, twice daily (e.g., 4-month puppy = 20 min x 2)
- **Type**: Short walks, play sessions, socialization
- **Caution**: Avoid overexercise - growing bones are fragile
- **Frequency**: Multiple short sessions better than one long walk

### Adult Dogs (1-7 years)
- **High-energy breeds** (Border Collie, Husky, Retriever): 60-120 minutes daily
- **Medium-energy breeds** (Beagle, Bulldog, Poodle): 30-60 minutes daily
- **Low-energy breeds** (Basset Hound, Shih Tzu): 20-30 minutes daily
- **Type**: Mix of walks, fetch, swimming, hiking

### Senior Dogs (7+ years)
- **Duration**: 20-30 minutes daily, split into shorter walks
- **Type**: Gentle walks, swimming (low-impact), mental stimulation
- **Considerations**: Watch for joint pain, adjust based on mobility

## Feeding Guidelines

### Meal Frequency
- **Puppies (8-12 weeks)**: 4 meals per day
- **Puppies (3-6 months)**: 3 meals per day
- **Puppies (6-12 months)**: 2 meals per day
- **Adult dogs**: 2 meals per day (morning and evening)
- **Senior dogs**: 2 meals per day, possibly smaller portions

### Timing
- Feed at consistent times daily
- Allow 30-60 minutes rest after meals before exercise
- Prevents bloat, especially in large deep-chested breeds

### Special Considerations
- **Working/Active dogs**: May need 3 meals to maintain energy
- **Diabetic dogs**: Must eat at same times daily, before insulin
- **Overweight dogs**: Split daily portion into 3-4 smaller meals

## Medication Administration

### Time-Critical Medications
- **Antibiotics**: Give at same time daily; complete full course
- **Insulin**: Give with meals at exact times (usually every 12 hours)
- **Thyroid medication**: Give on empty stomach, 1 hour before meals
- **Anti-seizure meds**: Must maintain consistent blood levels - never skip

### Best Practices
- Use pill pockets or food to hide medication
- Check with vet if medication can be given with food
- Set phone reminders for time-critical meds
- Never skip doses without consulting vet

## Grooming Frequency

### Brushing
- **Long-haired breeds**: Daily brushing to prevent matting
- **Medium-haired breeds**: 2-3 times per week
- **Short-haired breeds**: Weekly brushing
- **Double-coated breeds**: Daily during shedding season

### Bathing
- **Standard**: Every 4-6 weeks
- **Active/Outdoor dogs**: Every 2-4 weeks
- **Dry skin prone**: Every 6-8 weeks with moisturizing shampoo
- **Caution**: Over-bathing strips natural oils

### Nail Trimming
- **Frequency**: Every 3-4 weeks
- **Signs needed**: Nails click on floor when walking
- **Caution**: Cut before the quick (blood vessel)

### Ear Cleaning
- **Floppy ears**: Weekly inspection, clean as needed
- **Upright ears**: Bi-weekly inspection
- **After swimming**: Always dry ears to prevent infection

## Mental Enrichment

### Daily Mental Exercise
- **Importance**: Prevents boredom, reduces destructive behavior
- **Duration**: 15-30 minutes daily
- **Activities**: Puzzle toys, scent work, training sessions, food-dispensing toys

### Training Sessions
- **Frequency**: 2-3 short sessions (5-10 minutes) daily
- **Benefits**: Mental stimulation, bonding, behavior reinforcement
- **Best time**: Before meals (motivated by food rewards)

## Health Monitoring

### Daily Checks
- Appetite changes
- Water intake
- Energy levels
- Bathroom habits
- Limping or pain signs

### Priority Scheduling
1. **Critical (Never Skip)**: Medications (especially insulin, seizure meds)
2. **High Priority**: Feeding times, potty breaks
3. **Important**: Exercise, mental stimulation
4. **Routine**: Grooming, training
5. **Flexible**: Play time, enrichment activities

## Special Needs Considerations

### Diabetic Dogs
- Meals and insulin must be at exact same times daily
- Exercise at consistent times to maintain glucose levels
- Monitor for hypoglycemia signs (weakness, confusion)

### Senior Dogs with Arthritis
- Gentle exercise critical - prevents joint stiffening
- Multiple short walks better than one long walk
- Swimming provides exercise without joint stress

### Anxious/Fearful Dogs
- Consistent routine reduces anxiety
- Mental enrichment activities reduce stress
- Regular exercise essential for anxiety management

### Post-Surgery Recovery
- Follow vet's specific exercise restrictions
- Short potty breaks only during initial recovery
- Gradually increase activity as healing progresses
